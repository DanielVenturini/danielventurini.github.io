The following words are very easy. They just lose a single e or a single o. So, the main difference between the infinitive tense and the simple past tense is the sound of ee /i/ becomes e /e/ or the sound of oo /u/ becomes o /o/.

For example, the pronounciation of breed is /briːd/, but the pronounciation of its past tense bred is /bred/.
  1. My arms (bleed) a lot of blood when I fell at School.
  2. The Terriers (breed) five doggy last month.
  3. My sister (choose) a shirt from the many in his wardrobe.
  4. You already (feed) the animals early! Don't you remember?.
  5. I can't believe Marcus really (meet) Selena Gomes in Dubai two days ago.
  6. It was my fault. I (shoot) the arrow in the Mary's mirror.
